# Finance, AI, and the Future of Risk Detection

I am Emanuel Telles Chaves, a Finance and Economics student fascinated by how technology is changing the way we understand and manage financial risk. I have always been drawn to problems where data tells a story that intuition alone cannot — and finance is full of those problems.

What excites me most about AI in finance is not the hype around it, but the very concrete ways it is already changing fraud detection and risk management. I have been following how banks like JPMorgan and fintech companies like Stripe use machine learning models trained on hundreds of millions of transactions to catch fraud patterns in milliseconds — patterns no compliance team could spot manually. The fact that these systems get smarter with every transaction, adapting to new fraud schemes in near real time, is what makes this area feel genuinely transformative to me, not just another tech buzzword.

**Three ways I see AI making a real difference in financial risk and fraud detection:**
- **Real-time transaction anomaly detection:** Models trained on a customer's historical behavior can flag a suspicious charge — say, a $4,000 wire transfer at 2am from an unfamiliar location — before it clears, dramatically reducing losses from account takeover fraud.
- **Dynamic credit risk scoring:** Instead of relying solely on FICO scores, AI can incorporate alternative data like rent payment history or cash flow patterns to build a more accurate picture of creditworthiness, which also helps underserved borrowers access credit they rightfully qualify for.
- **Automated compliance monitoring (RegTech):** NLP models can scan internal communications and flag language that signals potential market manipulation or regulatory violations, reducing both legal exposure and the cost of manual compliance reviews.
