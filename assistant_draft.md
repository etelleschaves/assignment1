My name is Emanuel Telles Chaves, and I am a Finance and Economics student with a strong interest in how emerging technologies are reshaping the financial industry. I enjoy exploring the intersection of quantitative analysis and real-world financial systems, and I am always looking for ways to apply new tools to complex economic problems.

The convergence of finance and artificial intelligence is one of the most exciting developments in the industry today, particularly in the area of risk and fraud detection. AI systems can analyze vast amounts of transactional data in real time, identifying patterns and anomalies that would be impossible for human analysts to catch at scale. This synergy has the potential to make financial systems more secure, efficient, and fair — which is why I find it such a compelling area of study.

**Three ways AI can be useful in financial risk and fraud detection:**
- **Anomaly detection in transactions:** Machine learning models can flag unusual spending patterns or account behaviors that deviate from a user's baseline, catching fraud in real time.
- **Credit risk assessment:** AI can evaluate creditworthiness more accurately by incorporating non-traditional data sources, reducing default risk while expanding access to credit.
- **Regulatory compliance (RegTech):** Natural language processing models can monitor communications and transactions for compliance violations, reducing legal and reputational risk for financial institutions.
